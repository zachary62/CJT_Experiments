I'm currently a Ph.D student at Columbia University advised by Eugene Wu.

I'm currently collaborating with Azure to build an in-database ML system.

Elevator Pitch of my research projects: Suppose you are a data scientist in a enterprise. In the data warehouse/data lake, you find many tables useful for you analytics. The tables may look like this:

,

where these tables are nested together in strange ways. Suppose you want to train a Xgboost model over it. You have to integrate these tables into one using join, the most expensive operator. After the model finishes training, you want to further performs interactive queries like checking the fairness of the model or adding latest data to these tables, which requires you to redo the joins again and again and again.

My research projects apply optimizations to data analytics over tables without materializing join and make them interactive & iterative.

The key insight is to apply many interesting theoretic ideas from PGM (Probalistic Graphical Model) to DBMS.
